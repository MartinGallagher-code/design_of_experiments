Plackett-Burman Design

Water Heater Efficiency

Plackett-Burman screening of thermostat setting, tank insulation, pipe insulation, recirculation timer, and inlet temperature for energy savings and hot water availability

Summary

This experiment investigates water heater efficiency. Plackett-Burman screening of thermostat setting, tank insulation, pipe insulation, recirculation timer, and inlet temperature for energy savings and hot water availability.

The design varies 5 factors: thermostat c (C), ranging from 48 to 65, tank r value (R-value), ranging from 6 to 18, pipe insulation (bool), ranging from 0 to 1, recirc timer (bool), ranging from 0 to 1, and inlet temp c (C), ranging from 5 to 20. The goal is to optimize 2 responses: monthly kwh (kWh) (minimize) and availability pct (%) (maximize). Fixed conditions held constant across all runs include tank size L = 200, household size = 4.

A Plackett-Burman screening design was used to efficiently test 5 factors in only 8 runs. This design assumes interactions are negligible and focuses on identifying the most influential main effects.

Key Findings

For monthly kwh, the most influential factors were inlet temp c (39.8%), tank r value (30.8%), pipe insulation (19.2%). The best observed value was 290.0 (at thermostat c = 65, tank r value = 18, pipe insulation = 1).

For availability pct, the most influential factors were thermostat c (51.9%), tank r value (19.0%), pipe insulation (13.9%). The best observed value was 100.0 (at thermostat c = 48, tank r value = 18, pipe insulation = 0).

Recommended Next Steps

  • Follow up with a response surface design (CCD or Box-Behnken) on the top 3–4 factors to model curvature and find the true optimum.
  • Consider whether any fixed factors should be varied in a future study.
  • The screening results can guide factor reduction — drop factors contributing less than 5% and re-run with a smaller, more focused design.

Physical Home & Environment Test? Use the Manual Workflow

Home and environmental experiments involve real weather conditions, thermal properties, and physical installations that must be measured in situ. The simulation script above is for demonstration. For your real experiments, use record, status, and export-worksheet.

After each experiment, record the measurements $ python doe.py record --config use_cases/132_water_heater_efficiency/config.json --run 1 # 4. Peek at partial results as you go $ python doe.py analyze --config use_cases/132_water_heater_efficiency/config.json --partial # 5. Full analysis when all runs are complete $ python doe.py analyze --config use_cases/132_water_heater_efficiency/config.json $ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/132_water_heater_efficiency/config.json $ python doe.py report --config use_cases/132_water_heater_efficiency/config.json \ --output report.html

Works Without a Test Script

The analysis pipeline doesn't care how results were produced. Record your measurements with record, and the full analysis, optimization, and reporting pipeline works identically. Use --partial to get early insights before all runs are complete.
