Box-Behnken Design

Car Wash Quality Optimization

Box-Behnken design to maximize wash quality and minimize water usage by tuning water pressure, soap concentration, and rinse duration

Summary

This experiment investigates car wash quality optimization. Box-Behnken design to maximize wash quality and minimize water usage by tuning water pressure, soap concentration, and rinse duration.

The design varies 3 factors: water pressure (psi), ranging from 800 to 2000, soap dilution (ratio), ranging from 1 to 10, and rinse sec (sec), ranging from 30 to 120. The goal is to optimize 2 responses: clean score (pts) (maximize) and water liters (L) (minimize). Fixed conditions held constant across all runs include wash type = touchless, water temp = warm.

A Box-Behnken design was chosen because it efficiently fits quadratic models with 3 continuous factors while avoiding extreme corner combinations — requiring only 15 runs instead of the 8 needed for a full factorial at two levels.

Quadratic response surface models were fitted to capture potential curvature and factor interactions. The RSM contour plots below visualize how pairs of factors jointly affect each response.

Key Findings

For clean score, the most influential factors were soap dilution (43.1%), water pressure (42.1%), rinse sec (14.8%). The best observed value was 8.1 (at water pressure = 800, soap dilution = 10, rinse sec = 75).

For water liters, the most influential factors were soap dilution (60.3%), water pressure (27.8%), rinse sec (11.8%). The best observed value was 53.0 (at water pressure = 1400, soap dilution = 1, rinse sec = 120).

Recommended Next Steps

  • Run confirmation experiments at the predicted optimal settings to validate the model.
  • Consider whether any fixed factors should be varied in a future study.

Step-by-Step Workflow

1

Preview the design

Terminal
$ python doe.py info --config use_cases/120_car_wash_quality/config.json
2

Generate the runner script

Terminal
$ python doe.py generate --config use_cases/120_car_wash_quality/config.json \ --output use_cases/120_car_wash_quality/results/run.sh --seed 42
3

Execute the experiments

Terminal
$ bash use_cases/120_car_wash_quality/results/run.sh
4

Analyze results

Terminal
$ python doe.py analyze --config use_cases/120_car_wash_quality/config.json
5

Get optimization recommendations

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/120_car_wash_quality/config.json
6

Multi-objective optimization

With 2 competing responses, use --multi to find the best compromise via Derringer–Suich desirability.

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/120_car_wash_quality/config.json --multi
7

Generate the HTML report

Terminal
$ python doe.py report --config use_cases/120_car_wash_quality/config.json \ --output use_cases/120_car_wash_quality/results/report.html
