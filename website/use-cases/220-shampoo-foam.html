Central Composite Design

Shampoo Foaming & Cleansing

Central composite design to maximize foam volume and cleansing power while minimizing scalp dryness by tuning surfactant blend, pH, and viscosity

Summary

This experiment investigates shampoo foaming & cleansing. Central composite design to maximize foam volume and cleansing power while minimizing scalp dryness by tuning surfactant blend, pH, and viscosity.

The design varies 3 factors: surfactant pct (%), ranging from 8 to 18, ph level (pH), ranging from 4.5 to 6.5, and viscosity cp (cP), ranging from 2000 to 8000. The goal is to optimize 2 responses: foam score (pts) (maximize) and scalp dryness (pts) (minimize). Fixed conditions held constant across all runs include fragrance = floral, preservative = phenoxyethanol.

A Central Composite Design (CCD) was selected to fit a full quadratic response surface model, including curvature and interaction effects. With 3 factors this produces 22 runs including center points and axial (star) points that extend beyond the factorial range.

Quadratic response surface models were fitted to capture potential curvature and factor interactions. The RSM contour plots below visualize how pairs of factors jointly affect each response.

Key Findings

For foam score, the most influential factors were ph level (77.5%), surfactant pct (16.0%), viscosity cp (6.5%). The best observed value was 8.2 (at surfactant pct = 22.1287, ph level = 5.5, viscosity cp = 5000).

For scalp dryness, the most influential factors were ph level (44.4%), surfactant pct (35.6%), viscosity cp (20.0%). The best observed value was 2.8 (at surfactant pct = 18, ph level = 6.5, viscosity cp = 2000).

Recommended Next Steps

  • Run confirmation experiments at the predicted optimal settings to validate the model.
  • Consider whether any fixed factors should be varied in a future study.

Step-by-Step Workflow

1

Preview the design

Terminal
$ python doe.py info --config use_cases/220_shampoo_foam/config.json
2

Generate the runner script

Terminal
$ python doe.py generate --config use_cases/220_shampoo_foam/config.json \ --output use_cases/220_shampoo_foam/results/run.sh --seed 42
3

Execute the experiments

Terminal
$ bash use_cases/220_shampoo_foam/results/run.sh
4

Analyze results

Terminal
$ python doe.py analyze --config use_cases/220_shampoo_foam/config.json
5

Get optimization recommendations

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/220_shampoo_foam/config.json
6

Multi-objective optimization

With 2 competing responses, use --multi to find the best compromise via Derringer–Suich desirability.

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/220_shampoo_foam/config.json --multi
7

Generate the HTML report

Terminal
$ python doe.py report --config use_cases/220_shampoo_foam/config.json \ --output use_cases/220_shampoo_foam/results/report.html
