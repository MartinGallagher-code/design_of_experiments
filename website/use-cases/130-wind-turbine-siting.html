Box-Behnken Design

Small Wind Turbine Siting

Box-Behnken design to maximize annual energy output and minimize noise by tuning tower height, rotor diameter, and distance from obstacles

Summary

This experiment investigates small wind turbine siting. Box-Behnken design to maximize annual energy output and minimize noise by tuning tower height, rotor diameter, and distance from obstacles.

The design varies 3 factors: tower height m (m), ranging from 10 to 30, rotor diam m (m), ranging from 2 to 7, and obstacle dist m (m), ranging from 20 to 100. The goal is to optimize 2 responses: annual kwh (kWh) (maximize) and noise dba (dBA) (minimize). Fixed conditions held constant across all runs include avg wind speed = 5.5m/s, terrain = suburban.

A Box-Behnken design was chosen because it efficiently fits quadratic models with 3 continuous factors while avoiding extreme corner combinations — requiring only 15 runs instead of the 8 needed for a full factorial at two levels.

Quadratic response surface models were fitted to capture potential curvature and factor interactions. The RSM contour plots below visualize how pairs of factors jointly affect each response.

Key Findings

For annual kwh, the most influential factors were tower height m (54.1%), rotor diam m (40.6%), obstacle dist m (5.4%). The best observed value was 4648.0 (at tower height m = 30, rotor diam m = 4.5, obstacle dist m = 20).

For noise dba, the most influential factors were obstacle dist m (59.3%), rotor diam m (29.8%), tower height m (11.0%). The best observed value was 32.0 (at tower height m = 20, rotor diam m = 4.5, obstacle dist m = 60).

Recommended Next Steps

  • Run confirmation experiments at the predicted optimal settings to validate the model.
  • Consider whether any fixed factors should be varied in a future study.

Step-by-Step Workflow

1

Preview the design

Terminal
$ python doe.py info --config use_cases/130_wind_turbine_siting/config.json
2

Generate the runner script

Terminal
$ python doe.py generate --config use_cases/130_wind_turbine_siting/config.json \ --output use_cases/130_wind_turbine_siting/results/run.sh --seed 42
3

Execute the experiments

Terminal
$ bash use_cases/130_wind_turbine_siting/results/run.sh
4

Analyze results

Terminal
$ python doe.py analyze --config use_cases/130_wind_turbine_siting/config.json
5

Get optimization recommendations

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/130_wind_turbine_siting/config.json
6

Multi-objective optimization

With 2 competing responses, use --multi to find the best compromise via Derringer–Suich desirability.

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/130_wind_turbine_siting/config.json --multi
7

Generate the HTML report

Terminal
$ python doe.py report --config use_cases/130_wind_turbine_siting/config.json \ --output use_cases/130_wind_turbine_siting/results/report.html
