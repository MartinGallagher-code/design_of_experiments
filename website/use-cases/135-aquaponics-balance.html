Box-Behnken Design

Aquaponics System Balance

Box-Behnken design to maximize fish growth and plant yield by tuning fish density, feeding rate, and water flow rate

Summary

This experiment investigates aquaponics system balance. Box-Behnken design to maximize fish growth and plant yield by tuning fish density, feeding rate, and water flow rate.

The design varies 3 factors: fish density (fish/m3), ranging from 10 to 40, feed rate pct (%BW/day), ranging from 1 to 4, and flow rate lph (L/hr), ranging from 200 to 800. The goal is to optimize 2 responses: fish growth g (g/week) (maximize) and plant yield g (g/m2/wk) (maximize). Fixed conditions held constant across all runs include fish species = tilapia, plant = basil.

A Box-Behnken design was chosen because it efficiently fits quadratic models with 3 continuous factors while avoiding extreme corner combinations — requiring only 15 runs instead of the 8 needed for a full factorial at two levels.

Quadratic response surface models were fitted to capture potential curvature and factor interactions. The RSM contour plots below visualize how pairs of factors jointly affect each response.

Key Findings

For fish growth g, the most influential factors were flow rate lph (45.4%), feed rate pct (31.4%), fish density (23.3%). The best observed value was 36.7 (at fish density = 40, feed rate pct = 2.5, flow rate lph = 800).

For plant yield g, the most influential factors were flow rate lph (45.2%), feed rate pct (33.5%), fish density (21.3%). The best observed value was 103.0 (at fish density = 25, feed rate pct = 1, flow rate lph = 800).

Recommended Next Steps

  • Run confirmation experiments at the predicted optimal settings to validate the model.
  • Consider whether any fixed factors should be varied in a future study.

Step-by-Step Workflow

1

Preview the design

Terminal
$ python doe.py info --config use_cases/135_aquaponics_balance/config.json
2

Generate the runner script

Terminal
$ python doe.py generate --config use_cases/135_aquaponics_balance/config.json \ --output use_cases/135_aquaponics_balance/results/run.sh --seed 42
3

Execute the experiments

Terminal
$ bash use_cases/135_aquaponics_balance/results/run.sh
4

Analyze results

Terminal
$ python doe.py analyze --config use_cases/135_aquaponics_balance/config.json
5

Get optimization recommendations

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/135_aquaponics_balance/config.json
6

Multi-objective optimization

With 2 competing responses, use --multi to find the best compromise via Derringer–Suich desirability.

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/135_aquaponics_balance/config.json --multi
7

Generate the HTML report

Terminal
$ python doe.py report --config use_cases/135_aquaponics_balance/config.json \ --output use_cases/135_aquaponics_balance/results/report.html
