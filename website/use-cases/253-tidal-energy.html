Box-Behnken Design

Tidal Turbine Placement

Box-Behnken design to maximize power output and minimize marine life impact by tuning turbine depth, rotor diameter, and cut-in speed

Summary

This experiment investigates tidal turbine placement. Box-Behnken design to maximize power output and minimize marine life impact by tuning turbine depth, rotor diameter, and cut-in speed.

The design varies 3 factors: depth m (m), ranging from 5 to 25, rotor m (m), ranging from 5 to 20, and cutin ms (m/s), ranging from 0.5 to 2.0. The goal is to optimize 2 responses: annual mwh (MWh) (maximize) and impact score (pts) (minimize). Fixed conditions held constant across all runs include site = tidal_channel, tidal range = 4m.

A Box-Behnken design was chosen because it efficiently fits quadratic models with 3 continuous factors while avoiding extreme corner combinations — requiring only 15 runs instead of the 8 needed for a full factorial at two levels.

Quadratic response surface models were fitted to capture potential curvature and factor interactions. The RSM contour plots below visualize how pairs of factors jointly affect each response.

Key Findings

For annual mwh, the most influential factors were depth m (47.1%), rotor m (39.6%), cutin ms (13.3%). The best observed value was 765.0 (at depth m = 25, rotor m = 12.5, cutin ms = 0.5).

For impact score, the most influential factors were depth m (50.6%), rotor m (27.1%), cutin ms (22.3%). The best observed value was 2.1 (at depth m = 15, rotor m = 12.5, cutin ms = 1.25).

Recommended Next Steps

  • Run confirmation experiments at the predicted optimal settings to validate the model.
  • Consider whether any fixed factors should be varied in a future study.

Step-by-Step Workflow

1

Preview the design

Terminal
$ python doe.py info --config use_cases/253_tidal_energy/config.json
2

Generate the runner script

Terminal
$ python doe.py generate --config use_cases/253_tidal_energy/config.json \ --output use_cases/253_tidal_energy/results/run.sh --seed 42
3

Execute the experiments

Terminal
$ bash use_cases/253_tidal_energy/results/run.sh
4

Analyze results

Terminal
$ python doe.py analyze --config use_cases/253_tidal_energy/config.json
5

Get optimization recommendations

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/253_tidal_energy/config.json
6

Multi-objective optimization

With 2 competing responses, use --multi to find the best compromise via Derringer–Suich desirability.

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/253_tidal_energy/config.json --multi
7

Generate the HTML report

Terminal
$ python doe.py report --config use_cases/253_tidal_energy/config.json \ --output use_cases/253_tidal_energy/results/report.html
