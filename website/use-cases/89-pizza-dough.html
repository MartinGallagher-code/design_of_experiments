Pizza Dough Formulation

Full factorial of flour protein content, yeast amount, olive oil, and fermentation time for chewiness and bubble structure

Summary

This experiment investigates pizza dough formulation. Full factorial of flour protein content, yeast amount, olive oil, and fermentation time for chewiness and bubble structure.

The design varies 4 factors: protein pct (%), ranging from 10 to 14, yeast g (g), ranging from 2 to 7, oil ml (mL), ranging from 10 to 30, and ferment hrs (hrs), ranging from 4 to 72. The goal is to optimize 2 responses: chewiness (pts) (maximize) and bubble score (pts) (maximize). Fixed conditions held constant across all runs include salt pct = 2.5, water temp = 20.

A full factorial design was used to explore all 16 possible combinations of the 4 factors at two levels. This guarantees that every main effect and interaction can be estimated independently, at the cost of a larger experiment (16 runs).

Quadratic response surface models were fitted to capture potential curvature and factor interactions. The RSM contour plots below visualize how pairs of factors jointly affect each response.

Key Findings

For chewiness, the most influential factors were protein pct (41.5%), ferment hrs (27.5%), yeast g (17.6%). The best observed value was 9.8 (at protein pct = 14, yeast g = 7, oil ml = 30).

For bubble score, the most influential factors were yeast g (48.3%), oil ml (27.8%), ferment hrs (15.0%). The best observed value was 9.8 (at protein pct = 10, yeast g = 2, oil ml = 10).

Recommended Next Steps

  • Consider whether any fixed factors should be varied in a future study.

Step-by-Step Workflow

1

Preview the design

Terminal
$ python doe.py info --config use_cases/89_pizza_dough/config.json
2

Generate the runner script

Terminal
$ python doe.py generate --config use_cases/89_pizza_dough/config.json \ --output use_cases/89_pizza_dough/results/run.sh --seed 42
3

Execute the experiments

Terminal
$ bash use_cases/89_pizza_dough/results/run.sh
4

Analyze results

Terminal
$ python doe.py analyze --config use_cases/89_pizza_dough/config.json
5

Get optimization recommendations

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/89_pizza_dough/config.json
6

Multi-objective optimization

With 2 competing responses, use --multi to find the best compromise via Derringer–Suich desirability.

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/89_pizza_dough/config.json --multi
7

Generate the HTML report

Terminal
$ python doe.py report --config use_cases/89_pizza_dough/config.json \ --output use_cases/89_pizza_dough/results/report.html
