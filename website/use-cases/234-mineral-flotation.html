Central Composite Design

Mineral Flotation Separation

Central composite design to maximize mineral recovery and grade by tuning collector dosage, frother concentration, and pulp pH

Summary

This experiment investigates mineral flotation separation. Central composite design to maximize mineral recovery and grade by tuning collector dosage, frother concentration, and pulp pH.

The design varies 3 factors: collector g t (g/t), ranging from 20 to 80, frother g t (g/t), ranging from 10 to 40, and pulp ph (pH), ranging from 7 to 11. The goal is to optimize 2 responses: recovery pct (%) (maximize) and grade pct (%Cu) (maximize). Fixed conditions held constant across all runs include mineral = chalcopyrite, grind size = 75um.

A Central Composite Design (CCD) was selected to fit a full quadratic response surface model, including curvature and interaction effects. With 3 factors this produces 22 runs including center points and axial (star) points that extend beyond the factorial range.

Quadratic response surface models were fitted to capture potential curvature and factor interactions. The RSM contour plots below visualize how pairs of factors jointly affect each response.

Key Findings

For recovery pct, the most influential factors were frother g t (46.7%), pulp ph (28.9%), collector g t (24.3%). The best observed value was 82.0 (at collector g t = 20, frother g t = 40, pulp ph = 11).

For grade pct, the most influential factors were pulp ph (55.5%), frother g t (25.9%), collector g t (18.6%). The best observed value was 34.6 (at collector g t = 50, frother g t = 25, pulp ph = 9).

Recommended Next Steps

  • Run confirmation experiments at the predicted optimal settings to validate the model.
  • Consider whether any fixed factors should be varied in a future study.

Step-by-Step Workflow

1

Preview the design

Terminal
$ python doe.py info --config use_cases/234_mineral_flotation/config.json
2

Generate the runner script

Terminal
$ python doe.py generate --config use_cases/234_mineral_flotation/config.json \ --output use_cases/234_mineral_flotation/results/run.sh --seed 42
3

Execute the experiments

Terminal
$ bash use_cases/234_mineral_flotation/results/run.sh
4

Analyze results

Terminal
$ python doe.py analyze --config use_cases/234_mineral_flotation/config.json
5

Get optimization recommendations

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/234_mineral_flotation/config.json
6

Multi-objective optimization

With 2 competing responses, use --multi to find the best compromise via Derringer–Suich desirability.

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/234_mineral_flotation/config.json --multi
7

Generate the HTML report

Terminal
$ python doe.py report --config use_cases/234_mineral_flotation/config.json \ --output use_cases/234_mineral_flotation/results/report.html
