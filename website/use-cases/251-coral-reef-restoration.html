Box-Behnken Design

Coral Reef Fragment Restoration

Box-Behnken design to maximize coral growth rate and survival by tuning fragment size, depth placement, and spacing

Summary

This experiment investigates coral reef fragment restoration. Box-Behnken design to maximize coral growth rate and survival by tuning fragment size, depth placement, and spacing.

The design varies 3 factors: fragment cm (cm), ranging from 3 to 10, depth m (m), ranging from 3 to 15, and spacing cm (cm), ranging from 10 to 40. The goal is to optimize 2 responses: growth cm yr (cm/yr) (maximize) and survival pct (%) (maximize). Fixed conditions held constant across all runs include species = acropora, substrate = ceramic_disc.

A Box-Behnken design was chosen because it efficiently fits quadratic models with 3 continuous factors while avoiding extreme corner combinations — requiring only 15 runs instead of the 8 needed for a full factorial at two levels.

Quadratic response surface models were fitted to capture potential curvature and factor interactions. The RSM contour plots below visualize how pairs of factors jointly affect each response.

Key Findings

For growth cm yr, the most influential factors were fragment cm (44.5%), depth m (27.8%), spacing cm (27.8%). The best observed value was 4.6 (at fragment cm = 6.5, depth m = 15, spacing cm = 10).

For survival pct, the most influential factors were fragment cm (43.1%), spacing cm (39.4%), depth m (17.5%). The best observed value was 83.0 (at fragment cm = 6.5, depth m = 15, spacing cm = 10).

Recommended Next Steps

  • Run confirmation experiments at the predicted optimal settings to validate the model.
  • Consider whether any fixed factors should be varied in a future study.

Step-by-Step Workflow

1

Preview the design

Terminal
$ python doe.py info --config use_cases/251_coral_reef_restoration/config.json
2

Generate the runner script

Terminal
$ python doe.py generate --config use_cases/251_coral_reef_restoration/config.json \ --output use_cases/251_coral_reef_restoration/results/run.sh --seed 42
3

Execute the experiments

Terminal
$ bash use_cases/251_coral_reef_restoration/results/run.sh
4

Analyze results

Terminal
$ python doe.py analyze --config use_cases/251_coral_reef_restoration/config.json
5

Get optimization recommendations

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/251_coral_reef_restoration/config.json
6

Multi-objective optimization

With 2 competing responses, use --multi to find the best compromise via Derringer–Suich desirability.

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/251_coral_reef_restoration/config.json --multi
7

Generate the HTML report

Terminal
$ python doe.py report --config use_cases/251_coral_reef_restoration/config.json \ --output use_cases/251_coral_reef_restoration/results/report.html
