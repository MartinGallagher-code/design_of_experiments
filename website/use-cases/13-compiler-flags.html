Compiler Optimization Flags

Screen 7 GCC compiler flags to minimize execution time on NAS Parallel Benchmarks.

Summary

This experiment investigates compiler optimization flags screening. Screen GCC 13 compiler flags to minimize execution time and binary size for NPB BT benchmark.

The design varies 7 factors: opt level, ranging from O2 to O3, vectorize, ranging from off to avx512, lto, ranging from off to on, march, ranging from native to znver3, unroll, ranging from off to on, fast math, ranging from off to on, and pgo, ranging from off to on. The goal is to optimize 2 responses: exec time (sec) (maximize) and binary size (MB) (maximize).

A Plackett-Burman screening design was used to efficiently test 7 factors in only 8 runs. This design assumes interactions are negligible and focuses on identifying the most influential main effects.

Key Findings

For exec time, the most influential factors were vectorize (30.0%), fast math (24.6%), pgo (18.2%). The best observed value was 103.48 (at opt level = O2, vectorize = avx512, lto = off).

For binary size, the most influential factors were vectorize (32.3%), lto (26.3%), march (20.6%). The best observed value was 28.13 (at opt level = O3, vectorize = off, lto = off).

Recommended Next Steps

  • Follow up with a response surface design (CCD or Box-Behnken) on the top 3–4 factors to model curvature and find the true optimum.
  • Consider whether any fixed factors should be varied in a future study.
  • The screening results can guide factor reduction — drop factors contributing less than 5% and re-run with a smaller, more focused design.
