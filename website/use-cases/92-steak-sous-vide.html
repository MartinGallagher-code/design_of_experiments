Full Factorial Design

Steak Sous Vide Cooking

Full factorial of water bath temperature, cooking time, pre-sear, and rest time to maximize tenderness and juiciness

Summary

This experiment investigates steak sous vide cooking. Full factorial of water bath temperature, cooking time, pre-sear, and rest time to maximize tenderness and juiciness.

The design varies 4 factors: bath temp (C), ranging from 52 to 62, cook time (min), ranging from 60 to 240, sear time (sec), ranging from 30 to 90, and rest time (min), ranging from 2 to 10. The goal is to optimize 2 responses: tenderness (pts) (maximize) and juiciness (pts) (maximize). Fixed conditions held constant across all runs include cut = ribeye, thickness mm = 38.

A full factorial design was used to explore all 16 possible combinations of the 4 factors at two levels. This guarantees that every main effect and interaction can be estimated independently, at the cost of a larger experiment (16 runs).

Quadratic response surface models were fitted to capture potential curvature and factor interactions. The RSM contour plots below visualize how pairs of factors jointly affect each response.

Key Findings

For tenderness, the most influential factors were cook time (66.7%), sear time (18.5%), bath temp (8.6%). The best observed value was 7.9 (at bath temp = 52, cook time = 60, sear time = 30).

For juiciness, the most influential factors were cook time (65.4%), bath temp (23.1%), rest time (7.7%). The best observed value was 10.0 (at bath temp = 62, cook time = 60, sear time = 30).

Recommended Next Steps

  • Consider whether any fixed factors should be varied in a future study.

Step-by-Step Workflow

1

Preview the design

Terminal
$ python doe.py info --config use_cases/92_steak_sous_vide/config.json
2

Generate the runner script

Terminal
$ python doe.py generate --config use_cases/92_steak_sous_vide/config.json \ --output use_cases/92_steak_sous_vide/results/run.sh --seed 42
3

Execute the experiments

Terminal
$ bash use_cases/92_steak_sous_vide/results/run.sh
4

Analyze results

Terminal
$ python doe.py analyze --config use_cases/92_steak_sous_vide/config.json
5

Get optimization recommendations

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/92_steak_sous_vide/config.json
6

Multi-objective optimization

With 2 competing responses, use --multi to find the best compromise via Derringer–Suich desirability.

Terminal
$ python doe.py optimize --config use_cases/92_steak_sous_vide/config.json --multi
7

Generate the HTML report

Terminal
$ python doe.py report --config use_cases/92_steak_sous_vide/config.json \ --output use_cases/92_steak_sous_vide/results/report.html
